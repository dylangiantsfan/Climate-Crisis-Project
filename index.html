Save the Frogs!

They need your help!

Climate change has been highlighted as a particular threat to amphibians, being the key cause of declines in 40% of species. As many of these loved creatures are impacted, the Frog Protection Association seeks to spread awareness about our favorite creatures, fighting against climate change.

A Vital Species in Danger

Frogs and other amphibian species are already experiencing the devastating effects of climate change. Without action, it is very possible we will see catastrophic ecological effects stemming from the loss of these creatures.

The Science is clear, Frog populations are experiencing a global decline due to a combination of factors, including habitat loss, climate change, pollution, invasive species, and disease. Scientists predict that one-third to one-half of the nearly 7,600 known amphibian species, including frogs, will become extinct in the next ten years.

The faster we take action, the more likely it is we can save this wonderful species before it is too late!

Why Frogs Matter

Frogs play a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance. They consume large quantities of insects, including those that transmit disease and damage human crops. Due to their extreme sensitivity to environmental changes, they are also indicators of the general health of an ecosystem.

How You Can Help

There are many things that we can do individually to help our ribbity friends in danger. Avoid using any form of pesticides, as these can harm frogs directly and elimianates their main source of food. Create a frog friendly habitat in your yard, having a pond or birdbath gives a clean water source for frogs too hydrate and breed. Try your best to reduce your use of plastic and fossil fuels, as these contribute to climate change and pollution which damages frog's environment.
